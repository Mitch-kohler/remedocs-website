What We Check

A comprehensive accessibility audit, not a surface scan

We test for the issues that actually cause screen readers, assistive technology, and compliance auditors to flag your PDFs.

Tag structure

Verifies that the PDF has a complete, logical tag tree with correct nesting and role mappings.

Alt text

Checks that every image and figure has meaningful alternative text, and flags decorative images missing artifact tagging.

Reading order

Validates that content flows in the correct logical sequence, especially in multi-column layouts and complex designs.

Headings

Ensures heading levels are properly nested (H1 through H6) and that the document outline is navigable.

Tables

Confirms that data tables have proper header cells, scope attributes, and summary information for screen reader navigation.

Language

Detects whether the document language is set and whether language changes within the content are properly marked.

Bookmarks

Checks for a bookmark tree that mirrors the document's heading structure, enabling quick navigation in long PDFs.

Standards Covered

Tested against every major accessibility standard

Our checker validates your PDF against all three standards simultaneously and tells you exactly where each one fails.

WCAG 2.1 AA

Web Content Accessibility Guidelines

The international benchmark for digital accessibility. We test every applicable Level AA success criterion for document content.

PDF/UA-1

ISO 14289-1

The ISO standard dedicated to PDF accessibility. We verify tag structure, role mappings, and metadata requirements specific to PDF/UA.

Section 508

U.S. Federal Accessibility Law

Mandatory for federal agencies and contractors. Our report maps each issue to the relevant 508 requirement for straightforward compliance.
